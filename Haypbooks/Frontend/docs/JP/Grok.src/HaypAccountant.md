### QuickBooks Online: How It Handles Multiple Companies & Clients (Simple Explanation)

There are **two completely different ways** QuickBooks handles "multiple companies" — it depends on who you are:

#### 1. Regular Business Owner (You own multiple businesses/companies)
- Each "company" = separate set of books (like separate tenants in HaypBooks).
- **Subscription**: Each company needs **its own paid subscription** (e.g., Plus, Advanced). You **cannot** put multiple companies under one subscription.
- **How to manage/switch**:
  - Use the **same login email/password** for all companies (Intuit account links them).
  - When you log in, QBO asks: "Which company do you want to open?"
  - To switch later: Click **Gear icon (top right) → Switch company** → pick from your list.
- **No special "clients page"** — just a simple dropdown list of your own companies.
- **Example flow**:
  - You own Company A (already on QBO).
  - You buy a new subscription for Company B.
  - During signup for B, choose "Add to existing account" and use same login.
  - Now you have 2 companies, same login, easy switch.
- The invited accountant can access your company, but you can't access theirs.

#### 2. Accountant/Bookkeeper (You manage books for many clients)
- This is different — you get a **free special version**: QuickBooks Online Accountant (QBOA).
- **Subscription**: Each client's company still needs **its own paid subscription** (client pays, or you pay with discount and bill them).
- **How to manage**:
  - You have a **dedicated "Clients" page/dashboard** — this is the central list of all your clients' companies.
  - From there: See all clients, their subscription status, recent activity.
  - Click a client → instantly open their books (no logout/login).
  - Switch clients from a dropdown toolbar.
- **Example flow**:
  - Client invites you as their accountant (free for you).
  - OR you create the QBO company for them and add to your QBOA.
  - Client's company appears in your Clients dashboard.
  - You work on their books; they can log in separately if they want.
- This is perfect for pros managing 10–100+ separate companies.

#### Text Flow to Make It Clear (Step-by-Step Story)

**Scenario 1: You are a business owner with 2 companies (e.g., Cafe + Online Store)**
1. You sign up for QBO for your Cafe → pay subscription → create Cafe books.
2. Later, you want Online Store books → buy **another subscription**.
3. During signup, say "Add to existing Intuit account" → use same email/password.
4. Now: Log in once → QBO shows "Cafe" or "Online Store?" → pick one.
5. Inside app: Gear → Switch company → jump between them anytime.
6. Data completely separate (no mixing). You pay 2 subscriptions.

**Scenario 2: You are an accountant with 5 clients**
1. Sign up for **free QuickBooks Online Accountant (QBOA)**.
2. Client A buys their own QBO subscription → invites you as accountant.
3. Client A appears in your **Clients dashboard** (special page listing all clients).
4. Repeat for Clients B–E.
5. In QBOA: See list of all 5 clients → click one → work on their books.
6. Switch anytime from top dropdown.
7. Clients pay their own subscriptions (or you pay with accountant discount).
8. Clients can log in to their own company separately.

#### Who Needs a "Clients Management Page"?
- **Only accountants/bookkeepers** → They get the full Clients dashboard in QBOA to manage many separate companies easily.
- **Regular business owners** → No need — just a simple switcher dropdown for their own few companies.

**Yes — QuickBooks Online Accountant (QBOA) is completely FREE for accountants/bookkeepers.**

It's a special free tool from Intuit just for accounting professionals. You sign up once, and it becomes your "hub" to manage many clients.

Let me explain it super simply, step by step, to clear the confusion.

### Two Different "Accounts" in QuickBooks
1. **Normal QuickBooks Online (QBO) account**  
   - This is for **business owners** (the people who own/run the company).  
   - They pay a monthly subscription (e.g., $30–$200 depending on plan).  
   - This creates **one company file** (one set of books).  
   - The business owner is the "Primary Admin" — they control everything.

2. **QuickBooks Online Accountant (QBOA) account**  
   - This is **free** and only for accountants/bookkeepers.  
   - It's like a dashboard/portal.  
   - No subscription cost for the accountant.  
   - It lets you connect to **many client companies** (each client still pays their own QBO subscription).

### The Flow: How It All Connects

**Step-by-Step Story for a Business Owner (Owns the Company)**  
1. Business owner signs up for **paid QBO** → creates their company books.  
2. They become the owner/admin of that one company.  
3. They can invite an accountant:  
   - Go to Settings → Manage Users → Invite Accountant → enter accountant's email.  
   - Accountant gets email invite.

**Step-by-Step Story for the Accountant**  
1. Accountant signs up for **free QBOA** at quickbooks.intuit.com/accountants (or if invited first, the invite link creates it).  
   - It's free forever — no credit card needed.  
2. When a business owner invites them (or accountant adds client directly):  
   - The client's company appears in the accountant's **Clients Dashboard** (a special page listing all clients).  
3. Accountant clicks a client → opens their books directly (no new login).  
   - Switch between clients easily from a dropdown.  
   - Full access to fix/review books.  
4. The business owner still owns/pays for their QBO subscription — accountant just has free access.

**Example**:
- You (business owner) pay for QBO → your cafe company.  
- You invite me (accountant) → I accept in my **free QBOA**.  
- Your cafe appears in my Clients list.  
- I help with your books.  
- You can still log in separately to your QBO.

**If the Accountant Creates the Company for the Client**:
- Accountant uses free QBOA → "Add Client" → sets up QBO subscription (client pays, or accountant pays with discount and bills client).  
- Client gets login → owns the company.

### Key Points to Remember
- **Business owner**: Pays for QBO, owns one (or more) companies, can create their own books.  
- **Accountant**: Free QBOA, no payment, manages many clients' books from one place.  
- Clients' data is separate and secure — accountant sees only what they're invited to.  
- No "company inside company" — each business is a separate QBO file.

Now it's clear? QBOA is free for accountants to make their job easier with multiple clients.

### Why QuickBooks Structures Plans This Way
Intuit designs plans as **tiered/up-selling** to match business growth:

- **Simple Start** — Entry-level for solopreneurs/freelancers (1 user + accountant access).
- **Essentials** — Small teams needing bill management/time tracking (up to 3 users + accountant).
- **Plus** — Growing businesses with inventory/projects (up to 5 users + accountant).
- **Advanced** — Larger/more complex operations (up to 25 users, custom roles, advanced automation + accountant).

Key patterns in the image:
- **Core features** (track income/expenses, unlimited invoices/quotes, bank connect, GST/VAT, reports, progress invoicing) → Available in **all plans** (checked across columns).
- **User limits** → Increase with tiers (1 → 3 → 5 → unlimited/custom).
- **Advanced features** → Unlock progressively:
  - Manage bills/payments, employee time, multi-currency → Start from Essentials.
  - Recurring transactions, inventory, budgets, classes/locations → From Plus (with limits like 40 classes).
  - Unlimited classes/locations, custom roles/permissions, up to 25 users, data sync/restore, revenue recognition → Exclusive to Advanced.

### Why This Structure (Especially in QBOA Context)
- **Business growth strategy** — Start cheap/simple → Users upgrade as they add team members, inventory, or complexity → Intuit earns more over time.
- **Accountant incentive** — All plans include "plus your accountant" (free access for 1-2 accountants via QBOA). This encourages businesses to invite accountants early → Accountants (using free QBOA) push clients to higher plans for better features → More revenue for Intuit.
- **QBOA is separate & free** — The plans in the image are what **clients pay for** (business owners). Accountants get free QBOA hub to manage those client companies → No plan choice for accountants themselves.

This model works well: Low entry barrier → Sticky as businesses grow → Accountants become "sales partners" by recommending upgrades.

For **HaypBooks reference**: You could adopt similar tiers (e.g., Starter for solo, Team for multi-user, Pro for unlimited/inventory/advanced reports) — keeps pricing simple while encouraging upgrades.
