## Accounting Cycle Overview

### The 9-Step Accounting Cycle

#### Step 1: Collect Source Documents
- **Purpose**: Gather all financial transaction documentation
- **Documents**: Invoices, receipts, bank statements, contracts, payroll records
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Receipt capture, document storage, attachment features
- **Best Practices**: Digital document management, automated collection

#### Step 2: Analyze Transactions
- **Purpose**: Determine financial impact and proper classification
- **Analysis**: Debit/credit analysis, account classification, tax implications
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Transaction review, categorization suggestions
- **Best Practices**: Consistent classification, automated rules

#### Step 3: Record Journal Entries
- **Purpose**: Record transactions in chronological order
- **Types**: General journal entries, adjusting entries, closing entries
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Journal entry forms, recurring transactions
- **Best Practices**: Detailed descriptions, proper documentation

#### Step 4: Post to Ledger Accounts
- **Purpose**: Transfer journal entries to appropriate ledger accounts
- **Process**: Update account balances, maintain subsidiary ledgers
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Automatic posting, real-time balance updates
- **Best Practices**: Regular reconciliation, balance verification

#### Step 5: Prepare Trial Balance
- **Purpose**: Verify that debits equal credits
- **Process**: List all accounts with balances, calculate totals
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Automated trial balance reports
- **Best Practices**: Regular preparation, error identification

#### Step 6: Make Adjusting Entries
- **Purpose**: Record accrued/deferred items and estimates
- **Types**: Accruals, deferrals, depreciation, allowances
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Recurring adjusting entries, automation rules
- **Best Practices**: Consistent timing, proper documentation

#### Step 7: Prepare Adjusted Trial Balance
- **Purpose**: Verify adjustments and prepare for financial statements
- **Process**: Include all adjusting entries in trial balance
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Adjusted trial balance reports
- **Best Practices**: Thorough review, mathematical verification

#### Step 8: Prepare Financial Statements
- **Purpose**: Generate income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement
- **Process**: Compile data from adjusted trial balance
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Automated financial statement generation
- **Best Practices**: GAAP compliance, comparative reporting

#### Step 9: Close Temporary Accounts
- **Purpose**: Reset temporary accounts for next period
- **Process**: Transfer net income/loss to retained earnings
- **Haypbooks Tools**: Automated closing entries
- **Best Practices**: Proper sequencing, documentation retention

### Accounting Methods

#### Cash Basis Accounting
- **Recognition**: Revenue/expenses recognized when cash changes hands
- **Advantages**: Simple, matches cash flow
- **Limitations**: Doesn't show true profitability, timing differences
- **Haypbooks Setup**: Company preferences → Accounting method

#### Accrual Basis Accounting
- **Recognition**: Revenue when earned, expenses when incurred
- **Advantages**: Shows true financial position, GAAP compliant
- **Limitations**: More complex, requires adjustments
- **Haypbooks Setup**: Company preferences → Accounting method

## Chart of Accounts Setup

### Account Structure Planning

#### Asset Accounts (1000-1999)
- **Current Assets**: Cash, accounts receivable, inventory
- **Fixed Assets**: Property, equipment, vehicles
- **Other Assets**: Deposits, prepaid expenses, investments

#### Liability Accounts (2000-2999)
- **Current Liabilities**: Accounts payable, credit cards, loans
- **Long-term Liabilities**: Mortgages, long-term loans
- **Other Liabilities**: Deferred revenue, accrued expenses

#### Equity Accounts (3000-3999)
- **Owner Equity**: Capital contributions, retained earnings
- **Partner Equity**: Partner capital, distributions
- **Shareholder Equity**: Common stock, retained earnings

#### Income Accounts (4000-4999)
- **Sales Revenue**: Product sales, service revenue
- **Other Income**: Interest income, gains on sales
- **Revenue Accounts**: Detailed by product/service line

#### Expense Accounts (5000-5999)
- **Cost of Goods Sold**: Direct costs of products/services
- **Operating Expenses**: Salaries, rent, utilities, marketing
- **Other Expenses**: Interest expense, losses, depreciation

### Haypbooks Chart of Accounts Setup

#### Step-by-Step Setup Process
1. **Access Chart of Accounts**
   - Navigate to Gear icon → Chart of Accounts
   - Review default accounts
   - Add custom accounts as needed

2. **Account Creation**
   - Click "New" button
   - Select account type
   - Enter account name and number
   - Set account details and tax settings

3. **Account Organization**
   - Use account numbers for proper sequencing
   - Create sub-accounts for detailed tracking
   - Set up account hierarchies

4. **Account Maintenance**
   - Regular review and cleanup
   - Archive inactive accounts
   - Update account information as needed

## Transaction Recording Phase

### Source Document Collection

#### Document Types and Collection Methods
- **Sales Documents**: Invoices, sales receipts, credit memos
- **Purchase Documents**: Bills, purchase orders, vendor credits
- **Banking Documents**: Bank statements, deposit slips, checks
- **Payroll Documents**: Timesheets, payroll registers, tax forms
- **Adjustment Documents**: Depreciation schedules, adjusting entries

#### Haypbooks Document Management
- **Attachment Features**: Attach documents to transactions
- **Receipt Capture**: Mobile receipt scanning
- **Document Storage**: Secure cloud storage
- **Search and Retrieval**: Advanced document search capabilities

### Transaction Analysis & Classification

#### Transaction Analysis Framework
1. **Identify Event**: What happened?
2. **Determine Timing**: When should it be recorded?
3. **Assess Impact**: Which accounts are affected?
4. **Calculate Amounts**: What are the debit/credit amounts?
5. **Apply Rules**: GAAP compliance and company policies

#### Account Classification Rules
- **Revenue Recognition**: When earned, not when received
- **Expense Matching**: Match expenses with related revenues
- **Conservatism**: Recognize losses immediately, gains when realized
- **Materiality**: Focus on significant items, aggregate immaterial items

### Journal Entry Preparation

#### Journal Entry Components
- **Date**: Transaction date
- **Description**: Clear explanation of transaction
- **Debit Accounts**: Accounts to be increased/decreased
- **Credit Accounts**: Accounts to be increased/decreased
- **Amounts**: Dollar amounts for each account
- **Reference**: Supporting documentation

#### Common Journal Entry Types
- **Revenue Entries**: Sales, service income, interest income
- **Expense Entries**: Operating expenses, cost of goods sold
- **Asset Entries**: Fixed asset purchases, depreciation
- **Liability Entries**: Loan payments, accrued expenses
- **Equity Entries**: Owner contributions, retained earnings

## Adjustment Phase

### Accrual Accounting Adjustments

#### Types of Accruals
- **Accrued Revenue**: Revenue earned but not yet received
- **Accrued Expenses**: Expenses incurred but not yet paid
- **Examples**: Interest receivable, salaries payable, utilities expense

#### Accrual Entry Process
1. **Identify Accrual**: Determine what needs to be accrued
2. **Calculate Amount**: Determine the exact amount to accrue
3. **Record Entry**: Make adjusting journal entry
4. **Reverse if Needed**: Create reversing entry for next period

### Deferral Adjustments

#### Types of Deferrals
- **Deferred Revenue**: Cash received before revenue earned
- **Deferred Expenses**: Cash paid before expense incurred
- **Examples**: Prepaid rent, unearned revenue, prepaid insurance

#### Deferral Entry Process
1. **Initial Recording**: Record as asset/liability when cash changes hands
2. **Periodic Adjustment**: Recognize portion earned/incurred
3. **Amortization Schedule**: Track recognition over time

### Estimating Adjustments

#### Common Estimates
- **Bad Debt Expense**: Allowance for doubtful accounts
- **Inventory Obsolescence**: Reduction in inventory value
- **Warranty Liabilities**: Estimated warranty costs
- **Depreciation Expense**: Systematic asset cost allocation

#### Estimation Process
1. **Gather Data**: Historical data and current information
2. **Apply Method**: Use appropriate estimation technique
3. **Calculate Adjustment**: Determine adjustment amount
4. **Document Assumptions**: Record basis for estimate

### Trial Balance Preparation

#### Trial Balance Components
- **Account List**: All accounts with balances
- **Debit Balances**: Assets, expenses, losses
- **Credit Balances**: Liabilities, equity, revenues, gains
- **Total Verification**: Debits must equal credits

#### Haypbooks Trial Balance Features
- **Automated Generation**: Real-time trial balance creation
- **Filtering Options**: Date ranges, account types, classes
- **Export Capabilities**: PDF, Excel, CSV formats
- **Comparative Analysis**: Period-over-period comparisons

### Adjusting Entries

#### Adjusting Entry Categories
- **Accrued Items**: Revenues earned, expenses incurred
- **Deferred Items**: Prepaid expenses, unearned revenues
- **Estimates**: Bad debts, depreciation, inventory adjustments
- **Revaluations**: Asset impairments, inventory write-downs

#### Adjusting Entry Process
1. **Review Accounts**: Identify accounts needing adjustment
2. **Calculate Amounts**: Determine proper adjustment amounts
3. **Record Entries**: Make adjusting journal entries
4. **Update Records**: Reflect adjustments in financial records

### Financial Statement Preparation

#### Income Statement Preparation
- **Revenue Section**: Sales, service income, other income
- **Expense Section**: Cost of goods sold, operating expenses
- **Net Income Calculation**: Revenue minus expenses
- **Comparative Periods**: Year-over-year analysis

#### Balance Sheet Preparation
- **Asset Section**: Current and long-term assets
- **Liability Section**: Current and long-term liabilities
- **Equity Section**: Owner equity and retained earnings
- **Balance Verification**: Assets = Liabilities + Equity

#### Cash Flow Statement Preparation
- **Operating Activities**: Cash from operations
- **Investing Activities**: Cash from investments
- **Financing Activities**: Cash from financing
- **Net Cash Flow**: Overall cash position change

#### Haypbooks Closing Features

- **Audit Trail**: Complete record of closing activities

## Analysis & Reporting

### Financial Ratio Analysis

#### Liquidity Ratios
- **Current Ratio**: Current assets ÷ Current liabilities
- **Quick Ratio**: (Cash + Receivables) ÷ Current liabilities
- **Cash Ratio**: Cash ÷ Current liabilities

#### Profitability Ratios
- **Gross Margin**: (Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue
- **Operating Margin**: Operating income ÷ Revenue
- **Net Profit Margin**: Net income ÷ Revenue
- **Return on Assets**: Net income ÷ Total assets
- **Return on Equity**: Net income ÷ Owner equity

#### Efficiency Ratios
- **Asset Turnover**: Revenue ÷ Total assets
- **Inventory Turnover**: COGS ÷ Average inventory
- **Accounts Receivable Turnover**: Revenue ÷ Average receivables
- **Accounts Payable Turnover**: COGS ÷ Average payables

### Trend Analysis

#### Comparative Reporting
- **Year-over-Year Growth**: Compare periods sequentially
- **Quarterly Trends**: Identify seasonal patterns
- **Monthly Variations**: Track monthly performance changes
- **Custom Periods**: Compare any date ranges

#### Haypbooks Trend Analysis Tools
- **Comparative Reports**: Side-by-side period comparisons
- **Trend Graphs**: Visual representation of trends
- **Forecasting Tools**: Future performance predictions
- **Benchmarking**: Industry comparison capabilities

### Variance Analysis

#### Budget vs. Actual Analysis
- **Revenue Variances**: Actual vs. budgeted revenue
- **Expense Variances**: Actual vs. budgeted expenses
- **Profit Variances**: Overall profitability analysis
- **Department Variances**: Performance by business unit

#### Variance Investigation Process
1. **Identify Variances**: Compare actual to budget/forecast
2. **Determine Significance**: Focus on material variances
3. **Investigate Causes**: Root cause analysis
4. **Implement Corrections**: Address identified issues
5. **Update Forecasts**: Adjust future expectations
