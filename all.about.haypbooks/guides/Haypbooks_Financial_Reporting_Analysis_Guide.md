## Ratio Analysis & Metrics

### Liquidity Ratios

#### Current Ratio
- **Formula**: Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities
- **Purpose**: Measures short-term liquidity
- **Industry Standard**: 1.5:1 to 3:1
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better liquidity

#### Quick Ratio (Acid Test)
- **Formula**: (Cash + Marketable Securities + Receivables) ÷ Current Liabilities
- **Purpose**: Measures immediate liquidity
- **Industry Standard**: 1:1 to 2:1
- **Interpretation**: More conservative liquidity measure

#### Cash Ratio
- **Formula**: Cash ÷ Current Liabilities
- **Purpose**: Measures cash liquidity
- **Industry Standard**: 0.2:1 to 0.5:1
- **Interpretation**: Most conservative liquidity measure

### Profitability Ratios

#### Gross Profit Margin
- **Formula**: (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) ÷ Revenue × 100
- **Purpose**: Measures gross profitability
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (10-50%)
- **Interpretation**: Higher margin indicates better profitability

#### Operating Profit Margin
- **Formula**: Operating Income ÷ Revenue × 100
- **Purpose**: Measures operating efficiency
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (5-25%)
- **Interpretation**: Higher margin indicates better operations

#### Net Profit Margin
- **Formula**: Net Income ÷ Revenue × 100
- **Purpose**: Measures overall profitability
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (2-15%)
- **Interpretation**: Higher margin indicates better profitability

#### Return on Assets (ROA)
- **Formula**: Net Income ÷ Total Assets × 100
- **Purpose**: Measures asset efficiency
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (5-20%)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better asset utilization

#### Return on Equity (ROE)
- **Formula**: Net Income ÷ Owner's Equity × 100
- **Purpose**: Measures return on investment
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (10-30%)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better returns

### Efficiency Ratios

#### Asset Turnover Ratio
- **Formula**: Revenue ÷ Total Assets
- **Purpose**: Measures asset utilization efficiency
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (1-3 times)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better asset utilization

#### Inventory Turnover Ratio
- **Formula**: Cost of Goods Sold ÷ Average Inventory
- **Purpose**: Measures inventory management efficiency
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (4-12 times)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better inventory management

#### Accounts Receivable Turnover
- **Formula**: Revenue ÷ Average Accounts Receivable
- **Purpose**: Measures collection efficiency
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (4-12 times)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better collection

#### Accounts Payable Turnover
- **Formula**: Cost of Goods Sold ÷ Average Accounts Payable
- **Purpose**: Measures payment efficiency
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (6-15 times)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better payment management

### Solvency Ratios

#### Debt-to-Equity Ratio
- **Formula**: Total Liabilities ÷ Owner's Equity
- **Purpose**: Measures financial leverage
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (0.5-2.0)
- **Interpretation**: Lower ratio indicates less risk

#### Debt Ratio
- **Formula**: Total Liabilities ÷ Total Assets × 100
- **Purpose**: Measures total debt burden
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (30-60%)
- **Interpretation**: Lower ratio indicates better solvency

#### Interest Coverage Ratio
- **Formula**: EBIT ÷ Interest Expense
- **Purpose**: Measures debt service capability
- **Industry Standard**: 2.0-5.0 times
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates better coverage

### Market Ratios

#### Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
- **Formula**: Market Price per Share ÷ Earnings per Share
- **Purpose**: Measures market valuation
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (10-25)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates growth expectations

#### Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
- **Formula**: Market Price per Share ÷ Book Value per Share
- **Purpose**: Measures market vs. book value
- **Industry Standard**: Varies by industry (1-3)
- **Interpretation**: Higher ratio indicates market premium

### Customer Profitability Analysis

#### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- **Formula**: Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan
- **Purpose**: Measures long-term customer value
- **Calculation Methods**: Historical, predictive, cohort analysis
- **Application**: Customer retention and acquisition strategies

#### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- **Formula**: Total Marketing Spend ÷ Number of New Customers
- **Purpose**: Measures customer acquisition efficiency
- **Components**: Advertising, sales, marketing costs
- **Optimization**: Channel and campaign analysis

#### Customer Profitability Metrics
- **Gross Margin by Customer**: Revenue minus direct costs
- **Contribution Margin**: Gross margin minus variable costs
- **Net Profit by Customer**: After all costs and overhead allocation
- **Return on Customer Investment**: Profit relative to acquisition cost

### Product Performance Analysis

- **Direct Costs**: Cost of goods sold, direct labor
